How FlashFlow works
FlashFlow is built for fast, adaptive study. Import a deck, choose how strict or minimal you want the review experience to feel, and let the spacing system keep surfacing the cards that need more attention.
Import

Bring in almost any flashcard sheet

One CSV becomes one deck. FlashFlow accepts simple front/back rows, question-bank style Stem/Answer/Explanation, and cloze-style [[answer]] markers inside plain text.

Review

Reveal, judge, and move on

Study one card at a time. Reveal the answer, compare it honestly, then rate it with Again / Hard / Good / Easy. Those ratings shape when the card returns.

Learning Steps

New cards learn before they review

New cards follow your learning steps, Good moves them forward, Again sends them back to step one, and finishing the last step makes them a review card. Review cards that lapse go into relearning.

Typing

Use typing only when you need it

Typing mode is great for recall-heavy subjects. You can keep manual grading, or switch on Auto-rate typing if you want FlashFlow to estimate the result from answer closeness.

Panels

Make the workspace fit you

Use the four-dot handles to sort the side panels. The pin keeps a panel open; the hollow state lets it auto-collapse and open on hover.

Useful shortcuts: Enter or Space reveals the answer, 1 / 2 / 3 / 4 rates the current card after reveal, and ← / → steps backward or forward through recent reviews so you can fix a rating. Active decks appear in the calendar with their own colors.
